# MiroFish Prediction Prompt — Paste this into the "Prediction Requirement" field

Simulate the next 12 hours of NovaPay's incident response starting from Day 1, 03:00 UTC. Focus on these prediction questions:

1. How does the containment vs. availability conflict resolve? Does the team shut down the database connection immediately, or does the business pressure delay containment — and if so, how much additional data does the attacker exfiltrate?

2. When and how does external disclosure happen? Does the breach leak through the security researcher's Twitter thread, through a disgruntled or panicked employee, or through a controlled announcement? How do merchants, regulators, and the public react?

3. How do coordination failures across time zones, missing personnel (Alex Kim on PTO), and the skeleton weekend crew affect response quality and speed?

4. Does the CEO's instinct to "keep it tight" create downstream problems — delayed regulatory notification, loss of trust with the IR team, or legal exposure?

5. What does the attacker do next? Do they escalate to ransomware deployment, continue quiet exfiltration, or attempt to cover tracks once they detect containment activity?

Model all key personnel as agents with their described personalities, incentives, and relationships. Pay particular attention to the tension between the CISO (process-oriented, cautious) and the CTO (availability-focused, willing to bypass process), and how their disagreement affects the IR timeline.
